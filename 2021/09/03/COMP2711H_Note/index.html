Notes of solving problems

  • If you are required to proof $A \rightarrow B$ is true, then it suffices to show that whenever $A$ is true, $B$ is also true.

  • Sometimes some problems like ‚ÄúProof $\exists x \forall y P(x, y) \rightarrow \forall y \exists x P(x, y)$ is a tautology‚Äù , you don‚Äôt need to proof it by developing a series of logical equivalences.   • Example of proving certain number is rationalÔºö„ÄåProve that $\sqrt 2$ is irrational„Äç

    Let us prove it by contradiction.

    Suppose $\sqrt 2$ is rational, then there exists integers $a$ and $b$ such that $\sqrt 2 = \frac{p}{q}$, where $a$ and $b$ have no common factors.

    Then,
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    &2 = \frac{p^2}{q^2} \\
    \Rightarrow &2q^2 = p^2 \\
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    So $p^2$ is even, and we can deduce that $p$ is even. Suppose an integer such that $p = 2m$. Therefore, $2q^2 = p^2 = 4m^2$. Therefore, $q^2 = 2m^2$. And we can see from the equation that $q$ is also even.

    But $2 | p$ and $2 | q$‚Äôs both being true do not follow the premise that $p$ and $q$ have no common factors. Therefore, $\sqrt 2$ cannot be rational.
